It's been a while. Last year I was selling LED cables on Etsy — the fees were high and building them was too time-consuming. I shut it down and got back to what I actually wanted to build.

While that was happening, I was also designing the modules I've been sitting on for too long. That took longer than expected. Hardware always does.

I built this site to have somewhere to put it all: the specs, the process, the dead ends. If you've been following along from the early posts, the aesthetic should look familiar.

Here's where things stand.

## Distrans AR-1

A 6HP module that does three things at once: records your patch to MicroSD at 48kHz/24-bit, streams audio to a DAW over USB, and handles balanced I/O to studio gear.

One button. Long-press to record, short-press to mute, double-click to mount as a USB disk for file retrieval. No menus, no modes to remember. The idea came from losing too many patches because capturing them was a three-step process and the moment was gone.

Class-compliant USB — no drivers on Windows, macOS, Linux, or iOS. Currently on the second hardware revision. Firmware is quite far along.

[See full specs →](https://dxlb.nl/catalogue/distrans-ar1/)

## Conduit PDX-2

A 4HP power supply that runs your entire rack from a USB-C laptop charger. 3.0A on +12V, 1.5A on -12V. Switches at 2.2MHz — above the audio band — so there's no switching whine. Ripple under 5mVpp. Sequenced rails, TVS protection, short-circuit shutdown.

If you've ever tried to take a modular rig somewhere without hunting down the right power brick, you know the problem.

Currently in final hardware validation. Expected Q2 2026.

[See full specs →](https://dxlb.nl/catalogue/conduit-pdx2/)
