# Copy Guidelines

> Sources: Internal, 2026-05-01
> Updated: 2026-05-01

This document defines the tone and direction for specific content types across the platform. While the [Brand Voice](file:///home/dexter/Projects/Web/website/wiki/concepts/brand-voice.md) sets the overall vibe, these guidelines ensure each section serves its editorial purpose without "marketing bullshit."

## Catalogue: Product Focused

**Tone:** Spec-led benefit. Studio-grade, authoritative, and precise.

- **Direct Value:** Lead with what the product *enables* (e.g., "Power an entire case from a laptop charger").
- **Technical Backbone:** Every claim must be backed by a technical spec (e.g., "3.0A continuous output").
- **Terminology:** Use professional terms (ripple, pi-filter, TVS suppression) correctly and without apology.
- **Goal:** Build confidence through engineering excellence, not hype.

## Notes: Process & Thoughts

**Tone:** Translucent, investigative, and raw. The "Public Lab Bench" in action.

- **The Fails & Wins:** Document failures, half-finished prototypes, and dead ends alongside the successes. The "rough edges" are the proof of the work.
- **Narrative Arc:** Explain *why* a decision was made. Share the thinking, not just the result.
- **Visuals:** Use process photos—PCB revisions, oscilloscope traces, breadboard mess.
- **Goal:** To document a project's evolution for a technical peer.

## Feed: Status & Real-time

**Tone:** Low-friction, immediate, and personal. 

- **Micro-posts:** Short, tweet-like updates. No titles, no preambles.
- **Technical Snapshots:** Use for "wins" in the rack, quick bench observations, or deployment status.
- **Personal Touch:** It's okay to admit to "tinkering" or being "happy" with a result, as long as it's honest and direct.
- **Goal:** To provide a live pulse of the lab without the overhead of a full writeup.

## Mailings: Direct Updates

**Tone:** Personal but professional. Engineer-to-engineer.

- **No Sequences:** No "onboarding flows" or marketing funnels. 
- **Direct Communication:** Write like you're sending an update to a colleague. Focus on major project milestones or product availability.
- **Action-Oriented:** If you want someone to do something (e.g., "Check the new module"), tell them directly.
- **Goal:** To keep the core audience informed without cluttering their inbox.

## Legal: Transparent & Clear

**Tone:** Direct, plain-English, and trustworthy.

- **No Legalese:** Strip the "heretofores" and "notwithstandings." 
- **The "Why":** Explain clearly what happens with data, shipping, and returns. 
- **Trust through Clarity:** If a policy is strict (e.g., "No returns on DIY kits"), explain the technical reason why.
- **Goal:** To build trust through radical transparency.
