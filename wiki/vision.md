# Vision

## What is this project?

The Dexterlabs website — a personal site for a working software and hardware engineer. Part devlog, part product storefront, part portfolio. Not a startup; not a marketing site. A public lab bench.

## Why does it exist?

To give hobby projects structure, visibility, and a modest income stream. Eurorack modules, cables, and hardware experiments get documented and sold here. Software experiments get write-ups. The process — failures, dead ends, half-finished ideas alongside the wins — is documented as it happens. The site is the engineer's public bench, not a polished narrative.

## Who is it for?

Two overlapping audiences:

- **Technical peers and makers** — people who know what a pi-filter is, who build things, who appreciate honesty about failures.
- **Eurorack community** — synth builders and players who are the natural buyers of hardware Dexterlabs produces.

Both groups should feel addressed. A Eurorack post can mention 3D-printed brackets; a code post can mention the bench it runs on.

## What does success look like?

- Products are listed, buyable, and ship without friction (Stripe Checkout, EU VAT handled, SEPA QR on invoices).
- Notes document the real build process — readers can follow along and learn something.
- The site updates regularly enough to feel alive (feed, notes, mailings).
- Income from product sales covers the cost of the hobby and more.
- Agents using Vibin can build and extend the site without needing to re-derive context from scratch.

## Non-goals

- Not a hiring portfolio — the audience is peers, not recruiters.
- Not a generic marketing site — no hand-holding, no hype, no "excited to announce".
- Not a SaaS or startup — this is one person's side venture, not a product company.
- Not a community platform — no user accounts, no comments, no social graph.
