# Image Generation Prompts: Hardware & Eurorack

This document archives reusable prompt templates for high-end hardware and Eurorack product photography.

## The "Dune" & Feature Set (Brutalist Aesthetic)

Focuses on the brutalist aesthetic, recording features, and signal transmission.

### The Spice-Signal Recorder

> **Prompt:** 16:9 landscape. A brutalist-inspired product shot of a [Module/Hardware] floating in an orbit above a desert planet. The background is a hazy, orange spice-cloud nebula with faint, massive industrial silhouettes. The hardware is perfectly upright and proportional. Focus on the primary [Interaction Point, e.g., Red Button]-now a pulsing 'active' light-and the [Feature, e.g., MicroSD slot]. Subtle, glowing signal-transmission lines flow out from the hardware into the void. Matte black metal, industrial wear, low-glow, --ar 16:9

### The Heighliner Bridge

> **Prompt:** A 16:9 wide shot of a [Module/Hardware] inside the dark, cavernous void of a massive space vessel. The background is a deep, monolithic black with thin, vertical orange light-strips. The hardware is centered and upright, bridging the gap between two worlds. [Cables/Peripherals] float in zero-G, trailing off into the darkness. The LED indicators are dim and functional. Minimalist, brutalist, high-tech but ancient aesthetic, --ar 16:9

### The Arrakis Orbit

> **Prompt:** Macro-focus product photography, 16:9 landscape. The [Module/Hardware] is positioned in front of a giant, sun-scorched planet. Focus on the verticality of the hardware. The primary status indicator is a deep, ominous red. Visible signal-transmission artifacts (glitchy data waves) ripple across the stars behind it. The lighting is harsh and directional, catching the edges of the [Ports/Jacks]. No bloom, gritty texture, industrial sci-fi, --ar 16:9

## Glitch & Digital Void

For a more corrupted, data-centric aesthetic with minimal bloom.

### The Digital Void (Matte & Glitchy)

> **Prompt:** 16:9 landscape. An upright, slim [Module/Hardware] centered in a dark, glitchy starfield. **Minimal glow and no bloom.** The background features digital noise, subtle scan lines, and pixel-sorted star clusters. The primary controls are sharp and distinct. The LED indicators are colorful but sharp and dim, with no light bleeding. The aesthetic is 'corrupted data in deep space.' Proportional hardware, matte finish, 8k, --ar 16:9

### The Data-Stream Nebula

> **Prompt:** A 16:9 wide shot of a vertical [Module/Hardware] floating in a galaxy composed of digital artifacts. Instead of soft clouds, the background has horizontal data-glitch lines, color-fringing on the stars, and subtle VCR-style distortion. **Controlled, low-key lighting.** The LEDs are clear but do not cast a glow onto the surroundings. Realistic textures on the faceplate, centered and upright, --ar 16:9

### The Lo-Fi Cosmic Glitch

> **Prompt:** A proportional, upright [Module/Hardware] in a 16:9 frame. The background is a galaxy with high-contrast, 'crushed' blacks and digital compression artifacts. Incorporate subtle 'glitch art' elements like displaced pixels and RGB splitting in the distant stars. Primary controls and indicators are sharp and distinct. **No soft lens flares or bloom.** Cold, digital atmosphere, photorealistic hardware, --ar 16:9

### The Circuit-Bending Universe

> **Prompt:** Wide 16:9 landscape. A single [Module/Hardware] centered, maintaining its correct vertical dimensions. The galaxy behind it is distorted with motherboard-like traces and digital glitch patterns. **Low-glow aesthetic.** Primary indicators show sharp, pixel-like LED segments. The overall look is gritty, technical, and slightly corrupted. Proportional, 8k, --ar 16:9

## Technical Constraints & Anti-Squishing

Rules for models (like Nano Banana/Gemini) to prevent hardware distortion.

- **Orientation:** Always specify "perfectly upright" or "centered and vertical."
- **Proportions:** Use "proportional hardware," "un-distorted dimensions," or "narrow vertical aspect."
- **Layout:** For 16:9, specify "wide landscape background" to ensure the AI doesn't try to stretch the subject to the edges.
- **Lighting:** Use "matte," "no bloom," or "controlled lighting" to avoid over-saturated AI glows.
